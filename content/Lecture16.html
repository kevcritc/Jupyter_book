Nanotechnology & Biology#

  • Nanotechnology exists in living organisms.

  • Biological molecular machinery follows a ‚Äúbottom-up‚Äù approach.

  • Inspired the design of nanobiotechnology.

Chemistry of Life#

Water#

  • Polar molecule, essential for life.

  • Solvent for biochemical reactions.

  • Forms hydrogen bonds (up to 4).

  • Hydrophilic: Attracted to water.

  • Hydrophobic effect: Nonpolar molecules aggregate.

Carbon#

  • Forms chains, rings, and branched structures.

  • Carbon-rich regions are hydrophobic.

Molecules & Building Blocks of Life#

1. Carbohydrates (Sugars)#

  • Formula: (CH‚ÇÇO)n.

  • Functions:

    • Energy source (glucose via glycolysis ‚Üí ATP).

    • Structural role (polysaccharides).

  • Types:

    • Monosaccharides: Single unit (e.g., glucose).

    • Disaccharides ‚Üí Polysaccharides (e.g., starch, glycogen, cellulose).

  • Solubility: Water-soluble, crystalline.

2. Lipids (Fatty Acids & Phospholipids)#

  • Composed of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen.

  • Fatty acids:

    • Hydrophilic head, hydrophobic tail ‚Üí Forms micelles in water.

    • Functions:

      • Structural component in membranes (lipid bilayers).

      • Energy storage (minor role).

    • Types:

      • Saturated (no double bonds, solid at room temp).

      • Unsaturated (one or more double bonds, liquid at room temp).

      • Trans fats (see below).

Trans Fats ‚Äì What Are They?#

  • A type of unsaturated fat with a trans configuration at the double bond.

  • Two types:

    • Natural (small amounts in dairy & meat from ruminants).

    • Artificial (made by partial hydrogenation of vegetable oils).

  • Health Effects:

    • Increase LDL (bad cholesterol) and lower HDL (good cholesterol).

    • Linked to heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes.

    • Many countries have banned or restricted artificial trans fats.

3. Nucleotides & Nucleic Acids#

  • Main Roles:

    • Store genetic information.

    • Carry chemical energy.

  • Structure:

    • Five-carbon sugar.

    • Phosphate group(s).

    • Nitrogen base.

  • Types:

    • DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid).

    • RNA (Ribonucleic acid).

  • ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate):

    • ‚ÄúEnergy currency‚Äù of cells.

    • Energy stored in phosphate bonds.

    • ATP ‚Üí ADP releases 7 kcal/mol of energy.

4. Amino Acids & Proteins#

  • Structure:

    • Central Œ±-carbon.

    • Carboxyl (-COOH).

    • Amine (-NH‚ÇÇ).

    • R-group (Side chain).

  • Proteins:

    • Primary structure: Sequence of amino acids.

    • Peptide bonds link amino acids.

Summary#

  • Life is built on four main biomolecules:

    1. Carbohydrates (Energy & structure).

    2. Lipids (Membranes & storage).

    3. Nucleotides (Genetics & energy).

    4. Proteins (Structure & function).
