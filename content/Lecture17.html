🧬 From Bionano to Nanomedicine – Lecture Notes#

🧪 Molecules and Building Blocks of Life#

1. Amino Acids – The Basic Units of Proteins#

Each amino acid consists of:

  • A central (α) carbon

  • A carboxylic acid group (-COOH)

  • An amine group (-NH₂)

  • A hydrogen atom

  • A variable side chain (R-group)

💡 Think of amino acids like Lego blocks with different “side arms” – the R-group – that give each block its personality.

2. Protein Structure Hierarchy#

Proteins are built from amino acids, and their function depends on the structure:

Primary Structure:#

  • Linear sequence of amino acids

  • Linked by peptide bonds (between -COOH and -NH₂)

Secondary Structure:#

  • Local folding: α-helices and β-sheets

  • Stabilized by hydrogen bonding

Tertiary Structure:#

  • Full 3D shape of a single chain

  • Determined by interactions between side chains:

    • Hydrophobic interactions

    • Ionic bonds

    • Disulfide bridges

Quaternary Structure:#

  • Multiple polypeptide chains (subunits) form a larger complex

  • Example: Hemoglobin (4 subunits)

🔍 Key Insight: A protein’s 3D shape (conformation) directly affects its function – misfolding might cause diseases e.g. has been liked Alzheimer’s but be careful it might be sypmtom rather than a cause.

⚕️ Introduction to Nanomedicine#

What Is Nanomedicine?#

  • Application of nanotechnology in medicine for:

    • Prevention

    • Diagnosis

    • Treatment of diseases

  • Interdisciplinary field combining:

    • Physics, Biology, Chemistry, Engineering

    • Mathematics, Computer Science, Medicine

Emerging Fields:#

  • Nanotherapeutics

  • Molecularly targeted medicine

  • Immunotherapy

  • Synthetic biology

  • Lab-on-chip systems

  • Pre-clinical testing of nanoparticles

  • Genetic and tissue engineering

Why Use Nanoparticles in Medicine?#

  • Size: 1–100 nm (same scale as biomolecules)

  • Small enough to:

    • Interact with proteins, DNA

    • Enter cells and tissues

  • Used for:

    • Drug delivery

    • Imaging

    • Diagnostics

⚗️ Types of Medical Nanoparticles#

1. Lipid-Based Nanoparticles – Liposomes#

What are Lipids?#

  • Large molecules made from fatty acids

  • Phospholipids:

    • Two fatty acid tails + glycerol + phosphate group

    • Amphiphilic:

      • Hydrophilic head (water-attracting)

      • Hydrophobic tail (water-repelling)

    • Self-assemble into bilayers in water

Liposomes#

  • Spherical vesicles with an aqueous core surrounded by phospholipid bilayers

  • Biocompatible → safe for medical use

  • Common in medicine:

    • Doxil®

    • DaunoXome®

    • Ambisome®

Drug Loading:#

  • Hydrophilic drugs → aqueous core

  • Hydrophobic drugs → lipid bilayer

📘 Analogy: Like soap bubbles carrying medicine, liposomes can deliver drugs safely into cells.

🌍 Lessons from Nature#

  • Nature builds nanoscale machines using proteins, DNA, lipids

  • Nanomedicine mimics these natural systems

  • Combining physics + biology leads to smarter medical technologies

🧠 Nature has done 3 billion years of R&D — we’re just catching up.
