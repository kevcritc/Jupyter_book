Nanotechnology Essay Instructions#

Coursework Details#

  • Value: 20%

  • Deadline: Monday 2 pm on 10th March 2025

Assignment Overview#

You are required to write an essay about one application of nanotechnology. The essay should:

  • Focus on a nanotechnology application that is either already available or currently being researched.

  • Explain the physics and science behind the application.

  • Be written in your own words.

  • Be informative and accessible to a non-expert scientist.

  • Discuss safety, ethics, beneficiaries, and the future impact, including potential job creation, wealth generation, or broader applications.

  • The nanotechnology discussed could involve the following (but not limited to): nanoparticles, quantum dots, graphene, carbon dots, carbon nanotubes, nanowires, surface nanocoatings, atomic clusters, nanopores, or nanoelectronics devices.

Submission Guidelines#

  • Submit the essay via Turnitin on Minerva.

  • The essay must be between 1500 and 2000 words.

  • Ensure proper formatting and adherence to the required structure.

  • The essay must meet the English Language Competency Standards of the University of Leeds. Specifically:

    • Use clear and concise English.

    • Avoid grammatical and spelling errors.

    • Maintain an appropriate academic tone.

  • Refer to the University of Leeds English language requirements and guidelines for further details: English Language Standards.

Document Elements#

1. Title#

  • Choose an engaging title that reflects the content of the essay.

2. Introduction#

  • Provide a concise summary (approximately 300 words) of the key points discussed in the essay.

3. Background#

  • Identify the problem solved by the nanotechnology.

  • Explain why this technology is important.

  • Provide a detailed discussion of the physics and science underlying the technology, including:

    • How it works.

    • How it is made.

    • How it was discovered.

4. Discussion#

  • Highlight the potential benefits and impacts:

    • Who benefits from the technology?

    • What are its implications in 5, 10, or 50 years?

    • Can it create jobs or generate wealth?

    • Will it be applied elsewhere?

  • Critically evaluate the evidence:

    • Include opposing views.

    • Address safety and ethical concerns.

    • Identify potential problems.

5. Conclusion#

  • Summarise the major points.

  • Provide a strong closing statement.

6. Figures#

  • Include at least two figures:

    • Label and caption each figure.

    • Refer to figures in the text.

    • Acknowledge published figures where applicable.

    • Ensure high-quality reproduction or original artwork.

7. References#

  • Use at least six references:

    • Include four books or journal articles (not just website articles).

    • Ensure references are reliable and comprehensive.

    • Be consistent with citation style.

    • References are not included in the word count.

Mark Scheme#

  • Introduction: 10%

  • Background: 25%

  • Discussion: 25%

  • Style and Presentation (title, grammar, structure, figures, captions): 10%

  • Conclusion: 5%

  • Quality of Research (references): 25%

Important Notes#

  1. Late Submissions: University rules apply (5% deduction per day late).

  2. Plagiarism: Ensure your work is original and free from plagiarism, including self-plagiarism.

Use of AI#

In this assessment, AI tools may be used to:

  • Support your research on the topic. For example, answering questions, or defining concepts.

  • Provide you with feedback on sections of your content and ideas.

  • Support proof-reading.

Gen AI cannot be used in other ways to produce content for your assignment. You must include an Acknowledgement of AI use: -For example, ‚Äò I acknowledge that I used Co-pilot to support poof-reading and defining concepts.

Summary#

Your essay should provide a detailed, well-researched, and engaging exploration of a nanotechnology application, whether it is currently available or under active research. Be critical, concise, and comprehensive to maximize your marks.
